# Welcome to the core of our project! :wave: :earth_americas: → :seedling: → :rocket:

The first few months will be dedicated to **Phase 1: The Gathering**. This forum (found under the [Discussions](https://github.com/lets-build-something-amazing/project-ideas/discussions) tab on the top nav bar) is our dedicated space to brainstorm exactly what we are going to build together as a community. When we transition to **Phase 2: The Decision**, we will take the top ideas from this forum and put them to an official vote.

**A Challenge to the Community:**
While anything and everything is on the table, I'd love to challenge us to think of this as a sort of civic exercise. What if we could find ways to leverage technology that will directly benefit our entire city, county, or region? Let's dream big about local impact!

**The "Practicality" Principle:**
We don't just want to build something amazing; we want to build something practical. To ensure our ideas are actually useful, we should actively seek knowledge from the people "in the trenches" who do the work to help ensure our community is thriving every day.

> **Validate and Refine:** If you have a great idea but aren't sure it's practical, pitch it here! We can work together to find industry experts, get their feedback, and refine the idea until it perfectly fits a real-world workflow.

> **Discover the Need:** Notice a problem in a specific industry or local sector but don't know enough to suggest a specific app? Post the problem! We can go directly to the experts together and ask them what tools they wish they had.

How to use this forum:

**Like an idea?** Upvote the pitches you like and jump into the discussions!

**Need a template for your serious pitch? Copy and paste this into your reply:**

> **The Problem:** (What real-world problem are we trying to solve?)
> **The Solution:** (What is the app/tool we are building to fix it?)
> **The Civic Impact:** (How could this benefit our local city, county, or region?)
> **The Validation Plan:** (Who "in the trenches" could we talk to to ensure this is practically useful?)
> **Why it's great for beginners:** (Does it allow for simple contributions?).  *IME pretty much every project has plenty of opportunity for learning and engagement from beginners, but if you have a particular point you think will be relevant here please feel free to share!*
> **Potential Tech Stack:** (Got ideas for languages or frameworks? e.g., React, Python, Discord API)

Don't worry if your idea isn't perfect yet.  If others in the community are inspired by your pitch they'll almost certainly jump in to help you with brainstorming.
